What the model does

A synthetic data layer for quantitative research

Quantum model advantage

Designed for non-linear, heavy-tailed markets

The model is built to express market structures that are hard to cover with a single historical sample: latent regime shifts, path dependency, volatility clustering, fat tails and cross-asset transmission.

Regime superposition

Blend multiple market states into one generation process, allowing datasets to move between trend, reversal, compression and stress conditions.

Path diversity

Produce many statistically distinct paths from the same research hypothesis, rather than repeatedly testing against one historical realization.

Tail-aware simulation

Control jump intensity, volatility clustering and drawdown-like segments to study strategy behavior during rare or compressed market events.

Research use case

Same strategy, different data environment

A common commercial demonstration is to run a fixed customer-owned quantitative strategy on both historical data and FinTerra-generated synthetic samples. The point is not to claim future returns; it is to show that synthetic datasets can expand research coverage and reveal strategy behavior under additional market worlds.
